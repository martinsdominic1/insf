Our History

Igreja Nossa Senhora de Fátima · Brentwood Park, Benoni

← Back to Home

The story of this parish begins with the Portuguese and Mozambican families who settled in and around Benoni in the early 1940s, many of them mineworkers and their households far from home. To care for this growing community, a mission was founded in 1943 under Dom Teodósio Clemente de Gouveia, offering the first regular religious support to Portuguese-speaking Catholics in the area.

A Foundation Stone, Blessed in 1945

As the community grew, plans were made for a permanent church on this site. On 16 May 1945, the Bishop of Johannesburg blessed the foundation stone, marking the formal beginning of construction of what would become Nossa Senhora de Fátima.

Inaugurated in 1948

The church was completed and inaugurated on 13 November 1948. Designed by the Portuguese architect Carlos dos Santos, then resident in Mozambique, the building was built to seat around 300 worshippers. With its completion, it became the first church in South Africa dedicated to Our Lady of Fátima — a distinction the parish still holds today.

A Living Parish

In the decades since, generations of families have been baptised, married and farewelled within these walls. The parish continues to serve both the Portuguese-speaking community and the wider Catholic community of Benoni, carrying forward the devotion to Our Lady of Fátima that first brought this church into being.
